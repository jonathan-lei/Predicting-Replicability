<div class="sectionInfo"><h2 class="sectionHeading">EXPERIMENT 1</h2></div><div class="head-b"><h3>Method</h3></div><div class="head-c"><h4>Participants</h4></div><p><span class="sentence">Forty University of Plymouth undergraduate students (30 female, 10 male; mean age = 20.00 years, <i>SD</i> = 1.85 years) participated as part of a course requirement.</span></p><div class="head-c"><h4>Procedure</h4></div><p><span class="sentence">Participants, in individual sessions, first completed a scrambled-sentences task (adapted from Costin, 1969) involving 40 sets of four words each. </span><span class="sentence">By underlining any three words in a set, a sentence could be formed. </span><span class="sentence">For the neutral condition, the task contained 40 sets of scrambled neutral words. </span><span class="sentence">For the cleanliness condition, half of the sets contained words related to the theme of cleanliness and purity (e.g., <i>pure, washed, clean, immaculate, pristine</i>), and the other sets contained only neutral words.</span></p><p><span class="sentence">Immediately after the priming task, participants rated six moral dilemmas (as used by Schnall et al., 2008): “Dog” (eating one's dead dog), “Trolley” (switching the tracks of a trolley to kill one workman instead of five), “Wallet” (keeping money inside a found wallet), “Plane Crash” (killing a terminally ill plane crash survivor to avoid starvation), “Résumé” (putting false information on a résumé), and “Kitten” (using a kitten for sexual arousal). </span><span class="sentence">Participants rated how wrong each action was from 0 (<i>perfectly OK</i>) to 9 (<i>extremely wrong</i>). </span><span class="sentence">Participants subsequently indicated their feelings at the moment for the items <i>relaxed, angry, happy, sad, afraid, depressed, disgusted, upset</i>, and <i>confused</i>, using a 10.5-cm visual analog scale labeled “Don't feel at all” at one end and “Feel very strongly” at the other. </span><span class="sentence">All scales were scored by measuring in half centimeters from the “Don't feel at all” end, yielding raw scores ranging from 0 to 21.</span></p>
