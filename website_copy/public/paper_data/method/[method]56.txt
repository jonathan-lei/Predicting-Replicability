class="head-b"><h3>Method</h3></div><p><span class="sentence">Forty-three students from an undergraduate subject pool participated in the experiment for extra credit. </span><span class="sentence">The experiment used a mixed design with type of anchor (rounded, precise under, precise over) as a between-subjects factor and scenario (10—see Table 1) as a within-subjects factor. </span><span class="sentence">Respondents read the scenarios one at a time and provided a numerical estimate for each scenario before moving on to the next.</span></p><p><span class="sentence">Nine of the scenarios investigated price estimates (plasma TV, quick-melt cheese, beach house, pet rock, figurine) or estimates of attribute values (grams of protein in a health drink, pen life in kilometers, basketball shooting percentage, Hummer height). </span><span class="sentence">For example, the plasma-TV scenario stated:</span></p><div class="quote"><p><span class="sentence">Imagine that you have just earned your first paycheck as a highly paid executive. </span><span class="sentence">As a result, you want to reward yourself by buying a large-screen, high-definition plasma TV. </span><span class="sentence">As you browse through a store, you see a plasma TV from Sony that you like because of its attractive carbon-fiber finish. </span><span class="sentence">Its WEGA 50-inch screen has been rated by a panel of electronics experts as the “clearest and sharpest in the market today, because of a new technology that doubled its resolution and increased its contrast ratio to 4,000:1.” </span><span class="sentence">If you were to guess the plasma TV's actual cost to the retailer (i.e., how much the store bought it for), what would it be? </span><span class="sentence">Because this is your first purchase of a plasma TV, you have very little information with which to base your estimate. </span><span class="sentence">All you know is that it should cost less than the retail price of $5,000/$4,988/$5,012. </span><span class="sentence">Guess the product's actual cost. </span><span class="sentence">This electronics store is known to offer a fair price. </span><span class="sentence">Most of their items are priced very close to their actual cost because they compete on volume. </span><span class="sentence">The only reason that this particular plasma TV is expensive is that it uses a new technology, and the picture quality is outstanding. </span><span class="sentence">So the actual cost would be only slightly less than $5,000/$4,988/$5,012.</span></p></div><p><span class="sentence">A context-free scenario (i.e., “There is a number saved in a file on this computer. </span><span class="sentence">It is just slightly less than 10,000/9,989/10,011. </span><span class="sentence">Can you guess the number?”) was also included. </span><span class="sentence">We assumed this scenario was free of preexisting associations and that estimates would not be affected by inferences about the reliability of the anchor.</span></p>
