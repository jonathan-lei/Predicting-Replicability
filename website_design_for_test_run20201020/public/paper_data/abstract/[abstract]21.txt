<span class="sentence">The anchoring-and-adjustment heuristic has been used to account for a wide variety of numerical judgments. </span><span class="sentence">Five studies show that adjustment away from a numerical anchor is smaller if the anchor is precise than if it is rounded. </span><span class="sentence">Evidence suggests that precise anchors, compared with rounded anchors, are represented on a subjective scale with a finer resolution. </span><span class="sentence">If adjustment consists of a series of iterative mental movements along a subjective scale, then an adjustment from a precise anchor should result in a smaller overall correction than an adjustment from a rounded anchor.</span>
